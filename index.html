Academic Achievements During M.S. Program

Since starting the M.S. program in the summer of 2021, I've taken less than a week off from my full-time development job and have maintained a 4.0 GPA while taking two classes each semester. I've included a screenshot below of my Academic Record as of today, which excludes the classes that I'm currently taking.

I did learn early as a high school student that the objective with school is to learn and explore, not to get good grades - good grades tend to be the natural byproduct of excitement and interest, sort of like how wave function collapse in quantum mechanics is the natural byproduct of environmental interaction (I just learned this in CS 8395: Quantum Computing). I say this to emphasize that my GPA has less to do with an obsession with my record than a real appetite for growing as a technical professional. With this appetite, and a supplemental unwillingness to put my name on things that I'm not proud of, I've made and I continue to make a consistent effort to produce high-quality and often over-the-top work.

As a remote student with a full-time job, I unfortunately did not engage in any research projects with faculty, nor in any CS-related campus clubs or activities, but I feel my lack of engagement in those areas was counterbalanced by frequent eye-opening deep dives into unfamiliar CS topics like deep learning, formal verification with symbolic model checking, and the design of metamodels with WebGME to craft domain-specific modeling languages (DSMLs). I've spent many a morning, afternoon, and night reading, writing, and coding as part of this program under the close guidance from an admittedly overused Einstein quote: If you can't explain it simply then you don't understand it well enough.

Also, this program has undoubtedly helped me to become a stronger technical communicator - both as a speaker and a writer. Classes like CS6387 and CS6381 (both taught by Dr. Anniruddha Gokhale) posed challenges not only of designing and building things like complex publish-subscribe systems and cloud-based data processing pipelines, but also of providing recorded and live demonstrations of those systems with explanations of how they functioned. through I've produced a number of YouTube videos and articles walking through things like email security, distributed systems management, and cloud-based data pipelines Tying academics to work. migration of data.
